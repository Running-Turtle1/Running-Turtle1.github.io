发表于 2024-12-12 更新于 2024-12-13 分类于 英语 ， 英文练习

I’ve realized that I’ve been ignoring a fundamental trait of mine: efficiency + excessive simulation + error correction. These three are indispensable if I want to achieve great success in a particular field.

Efficiency comes first. In fact, efficiency + the right direction is enough on its own. However, we also need a methodology, and the simplest and most straightforward approach is excessive simulation + error correction. Just keep repeating the process. At first glance, this might seem to contradict efficiency—if efficiency is optimal, we should only focus on doing what truly matters. But the reality is, we often don’t know exactly what we should do. That’s why excessive simulation becomes the easiest and most effective mode of action.

Excessive simulation requires training resources, and these resources inherently determine the training direction. Incorrect training resources lead to an incorrect direction, which can be highly detrimental.

Take preparing for the CET-6 exam as an example. It’s clear that I’ve done some over-simulation here, but in uncertain situations, this actually turns out to be the best choice. For any specific word, I can’t be sure whether it’s useful or not. So, whenever I finish a CET-6 paper, I record all the words I don’t know from that test.

The results have been very noticeable. I’ve learned a large number of CET-6 vocabulary words and feel like my scores have improved significantly. In the past, I would take the exam completely unprepared, barely managing to score above 500. This time, I’ve set a more modest goal: around 550.

Even though I’m feeling confident, but in listening, cloze tests, and writing, there still present a lot of uncertainty. Plus, the CET-6 exam has an element of subjectivity, and luck can play a role in the outcome.

For now, I’ll write more essays and practice listening. I feel like this can be conducive to my strengths.

(Though honestly, I think cramming for the CET-6 is kind of stupid. But as an individual, I don’t really have a choice—grad school applications demand it. If I manage to score close to 600, it would definitely be a major advantage.)

我发现自己忽略自己的一个基本属性，效率+过量模拟+纠错属性，想在一个领域上得到很高的成绩，这三者是缺一不可的。

效率是第一位的，其实效率+正确的方向就可以了。但是还需要方法论，最简单粗暴的就是直接过量模拟+纠错，重复即可。乍看这也许跟效率相违背，如果效率最佳，显然只需要去完成自己应该完成的，但事实是我们往往不知道自己应该做什么。所以直接过量模拟就是最轻松的模式。

过量模拟就需要训练资源，训练资源实际上也就代表着训练方向，错误的训练资源就是错误的方向，坏处颇多。

以刷六级题目为例，显然自己有点超量模拟了，但实际上这是在盲目情况下的最优选择，对于某一个特定的单词，我并不确定他是否是有用的，只好刷到一套六级试卷，就把这套试卷不会的全部记下来了。

效果是很显著的，我学会了大量六级单词，感觉提分了很多。以前都是毫无准备，直接裸考，只能 500 分露头。这次目标定低一点，550分左右吧。

虽然牛逼吹完了，但是听力、选词填空、作文依然还是有很大的不确定性，何况六级是花卷，对于考生的运气还是有一定考究的。

最近，多写点东西，然后模拟听力，感觉优势在我。
